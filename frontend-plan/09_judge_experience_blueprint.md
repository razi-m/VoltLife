# 09 — Judge Experience Blueprint

The emotional arc, page by page: **scale → intelligence → trust → empathy → mandate.** Each beat names what judges see, what they must understand, and what they should feel. (Spoken script = docs/09; this is the screen's half of the performance.)

## Beat 1 — Mission Control, idle (0:00–0:20) · *scale*

**See:** dark map of India, hero counter at rest ("Lifetime: 0 batteries decided" pre-demo / cascade state after), quiet status dot, empty feed saying "Awaiting fleet ingestion —". **Understand (≤20 s, no narration needed):** this is a national command center for batteries. **Feel:** anticipation — the stillness is staged so the cascade lands harder. Hero line on screen: sub-caption "Autonomous battery lifecycle decisions — live."

## Beat 2 — Intake (0:20–0:35) · *credibility*

**See:** CSV drops, 5-row telemetry preview, schema check passing, 3 rejected rows *visible* in the reject report, LAUNCH. **Understand:** real data goes in; the system validates rather than swallows. **Feel:** "this is engineered." The visible rejects are deliberate — flaws admitted early buy trust for everything after.

## Beat 3 — The cascade (0:35–1:45) · *intelligence*

**See:** markers popping across India in grade colors, feed cards streaming — each card: grade badge, SoH, RUL *with range*, three reasons, then ASSIGNED chip + destination; arcs shooting to sites; counters climbing; Safety Saves count ticking with "Recycle — straight to certified recycler" cards in red. **Understand:** every single battery gets an individual, explained decision; dangerous ones are physically gated. **Feel:** awe, then — critically — *comprehension inside the awe*: the cards are readable at projector distance (IA rule: reasons-first, big type slots). A judge should be able to read ONE card aloud and have explained the product.

## Beat 4 — Aadhaar reveal (1:45–2:15) · *empathy*

**See:** one battery's passport — QR, the 21-char ID decoding segment by segment, the Life Story paragraph, the timeline from a Pune factory to a Rajasthan solar farm, SHAP bars showing *why*. **Understand:** identity + biography = a battery you could buy second-hand and trust; this is the BPAN future, working. **Feel:** the project's heart — judges quote the Life Story to each other six hours later (innovation #1's whole thesis).

## Beat 5 — QR to phone (2:15–2:25) · *trust made physical*

**See (on their own device):** the public passport. **Understand:** trust travels with the battery, not with our laptop. **Feel:** ownership — they're holding the product. This beat converts spectators into participants; nothing else in the room will do that.

## Beat 6 — Impact Center + India 2030 (2:25–3:00) · *mandate*

**See:** three hero counters land; mining-avoided kilograms; circularity dial at ~80; then the 2030 overlay — the batch's numbers multiplying to national scale against "India will retire 128 GWh by 2030." **Understand:** the demo was a sample of a mandated national problem, and the same system runs at that scale. **Feel:** urgency + inevitability — the last screen before scoring is a country-sized number with their own government's projection under it.

## Free-exploration contract (table judging)

Whatever a judge clicks must reward them: markers → passports, donut → grade story, deployments filter → the queue with reasons, a scanned QR from the printed card → live passport. Dead ends, spinners >1 s, and "TODO" states are scored failures — EmptyStates and 404-passport pages are designed (05_*) precisely so even wrong turns look intentional.

## Anti-goals

No walkthrough modals, no onboarding tooltips, no autoplaying explainer — a command center assumes competence. If a judge asks "what am I looking at?", Beat 1 failed; fix the screen, not the script.
